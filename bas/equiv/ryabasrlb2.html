Roliding Bead 2

In this section we will consider the second option of the original Roliding Bead problem. Namely, we will assume that there is no external input into the system once the thread holding the bead in a fixed position is cut.

Then, based on the law of conservation of angular momentum, as the distance of the bead from the axis of rotation grows, the bead's and the rod's angular velocity will diminish and the system will slow down. It means that the angular velocity \(\omega\) of the system after the bead starts moving along the rod is not constant but rather is a decreasing function of time.

Since the rod is massless, it has no moment of inertia and hence - no kinetic energy. Since there is no friction in the system, the law of conservation of energy is:

$$\frac {J\omega_0^2}{2} = \frac{J\omega^2}{2} + \frac {mv^2}{2}$$ $$\begin{equation}\frac {ml_0^2\omega_0^2}{2} = \frac{mx^2\omega^2}{2} + \frac {mv^2}{2} \end{equation}$$

where \(\omega_0\) is the angular velocity of the system in the initial (steady) state, \(v\) is the velocity of the bead relative to the rod, \(\omega\) is the variable angular velocity of the system at any given time.

From the original Roliding Bead problem we can use the Lagrangian-based equation (1) except that this time the angular velocity is not constant:

$$\begin{equation} \omega^2x = \ddot x = \frac {dv}{dt} \end{equation}$$

From (1):

$$\begin{equation} \omega^2 = \frac {l_0^2\omega_0^2 - v^2}{x^2} \end{equation}$$

Put (3) into (2):

$$\frac {dv}{dt} = \frac {l_0^2\omega_0^2 - v^2}{x}$$ $$\frac {dv}{l_0^2\omega_0^2 - v^2} = \frac {dt}{x} = \frac {dx}{dx} \times \frac {dt}{x} = \frac {dx}{x} \times \frac {dt}{dx} = \frac {dx}{x} \times \frac {1}{v}$$ $$\begin{equation} \frac {vdv}{l_0^2\omega_0^2 - v^2} = \frac {dx}{x} \end{equation}$$

Integrate both sides of (4), left - from zero to \(v\), right - from \(l_0\) to \(x\):

$$\begin{equation} \int_{l_0}^x {\frac {dx}{x}} = \ln x - \ln l_0 = \ln \Big( \frac {x}{l_0} \Big) \end{equation}$$ $$\int_0^{v} {\frac {vdv}{l_0^2\omega_0^2 - v^2}} = \frac {1}{2} \int {\frac {dv^2}{l_0^2\omega_0^2 - v^2}} =$$ $$- \frac {1}{2} \int \frac {d(l_0^2\omega_0^2 - v^2)}{l_0^2\omega_0^2 - v^2} =$$ $$- \frac {1}{2} \ln (l_0^2\omega_0^2 - v^2)\Bigg|_0^{v} = - \frac {1}{2} \ln (l_0^2\omega_0^2 - v^2) + \frac {1}{2} \ln (l_0^2\omega_0^2) =$$ $$\begin{equation} \ln \Bigg( \frac {l_0\omega_0}{\sqrt {l_0^2\omega_0^2 - v^2}}\Bigg) \end{equation}$$

Equate (5) and (6):

$$\ln \Bigg( \frac {l_0\omega_0}{\sqrt {l_0^2\omega_0^2 - v^2}}\Bigg) = \ln \Bigg( \frac {x}{l_0} \Bigg)$$ $$\begin{equation} \frac {l_0\omega_0}{\sqrt {l_0^2\omega_0^2 - v^2}} = \frac {x}{l_0} \end{equation}$$

Solve (7) for \(v\):

$$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{v(x) = l_0\omega_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac {l_0^2}{x^2}}} \end{equation}$$

Then:

$$\begin{equation} v(x = l) = l_0\omega_0\sqrt{1 - \frac {l_0^2}{l^2}} \end{equation}$$

and from (3)

$$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{\omega(x) = \omega_0 \frac {l_0^2}{x^2}} \end{equation}$$ $$\omega(x = l) = \omega_0 \frac {l_0^2}{l^2}$$

To find \(x(t)\) put (10) into (2):

$$\begin{equation} \omega^2(x)\times x = \frac {\omega_0^2l_0^4}{x^3} = \frac {d^2x}{dt^2} \end{equation}$$

Use the method of separation of variables to solve (11):

$$k = \omega_0^2l_0^4$$ $$\frac {k}{x^3} = \frac {d \dot x}{dt}$$ $$k \frac {dt}{x^3} = d \dot x$$ $$k \frac {dt}{x^3} \times \dot x = d \dot x \times \dot x$$ $$k \frac {dt}{x^3} \times \frac {dx}{dt} = \dot x d \dot x$$ $$k \frac {dx}{x^3} = \dot x d \dot x$$ $$-\frac {k}{2x^2} + C = \frac {\dot x^2}{2}$$ $$x(0) = l_0, \; \dot x(0) = 0$$ $$C = \frac {k}{2l_0^2}$$ $$\frac {k}{2l_0^2} - \frac {k}{2x^2} = \frac {\dot x^2}{2}$$ $$\dot x^2 = k\Big(\frac {1}{l_0^2} - \frac {1}{x^2}\Big)$$ $$\frac {dx}{\sqrt{\Big(\frac {1}{l_0^2} - \frac {1}{x^2}\Big)}} = \sqrt{k}dt$$ $$\frac {l_0xdx}{\sqrt{x^2 - l_0^2}} = \sqrt{k}dt$$ $$l_0 \int_{l_0}^x \frac {xdx}{\sqrt{x^2 - l_0^2}} = \sqrt{k} \int_0^tdt = \sqrt{k}t$$ $$\int \frac {xdx}{\sqrt{x^2 - l_0^2}} = \frac {1}{2} \int \frac {d(x^2 - l_0^2)}{\sqrt{x^2 - l_0^2}} = \sqrt{x^2 - l_0^2}$$

Consequently:

$$l_0 \int_{l_0}^x \frac {xdx}{\sqrt{x^2 - l_0^2}} = l_0 \sqrt{x^2 - l_0^2} \Bigg|_{l_0}^x = l_0 \sqrt{x^2 - l_0^2}$$

and:

$$l_0 \sqrt{x^2 - l_0^2} = \sqrt{k}t$$ $$l_0^2\Big(x^2 - l_0^2\Big) = \omega_0^2 l_0^4 t^2$$ $$x^2 - l_0^2 = \omega_0^2 l_0^2 t^2$$ $$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{x(t) = l_0 \sqrt{\omega_0^2 t^2 + 1}} \end{equation}$$

And the time \(t_s\) till separation of the bead from the rod is:

$$\begin{equation} t_s = \frac{1}{\omega_0}\sqrt{\frac {l^2}{l_0^2} - 1} \end{equation}$$

Putting (12) into (10) and into (8), we obtain:

$$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{v(t) = \frac{\omega_0^2l_0t}{\sqrt{\omega_0^2t^2 + 1}}} \end{equation}$$ $$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{\omega(t) = \frac{\omega_0}{\omega_0^2t^2 + 1}} \end{equation}$$

\(\blacksquare\)
