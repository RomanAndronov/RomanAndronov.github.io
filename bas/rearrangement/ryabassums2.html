Problem 2.1

Find the sum of growing consecutive powers of a constant integer base \(a > 1\):

$$\begin{equation} S = \sum_{i = 0}^n a^i = 1 + a + a^2 + a^3 + \ldots + a^n \end{equation}$$


Solution

Modify the structure of each member of (1) by multiplying it by \(a\):

$$\begin{equation} aS_a(n) = a + a^2 + a^3 + a^4 + \ldots + a^{n + 1} \end{equation}$$

Subtract (1) from (2):

$$-\,\left\{\begin{array}{lclllllllll} aS_a(n) & = & a & + & a^2 & + & a^3 & + & \ldots & + & a^{n + 1}\\ \\ S_a(n) & = & 1 & + & a & + & a^2 & + & \ldots & + & a^n\\ \end{array}\right.$$ $$\begin{equation} (a - 1)S_a(n) = a + a^2 + a^3 + \ldots + a^{n + 1} - 1 - a - a^2 - a^3 - \ldots - a^n \end{equation}$$

Rearrange the members of equal powers in (3) as follows:

$$(a - 1)S_a(n) = a - a + a^2 - a^2 + a^3 - a^3 + \ldots + a^n - a^n + a^{n + 1} - 1$$

and observe that all such members will cancel each other out except for the last two:

$$(a - 1)S_a(n) = a^{n + 1} - 1$$

and:

$$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{\sum_{i = 0}^n a^i = \frac {a^{n + 1} - 1}{a - 1}} \end{equation}$$


Problem 2.2

Find the sum of growing odd powers of a constant integer base \(a > 1\):

$$\begin{equation} S_{a^o}(n) = \sum_{i = 1}^n a^{2i - 1} = a + a^3 + a^5 + \ldots + a^{2n - 1} \end{equation}$$


Solution

Rearrange the structure of each member of (5) by multiplying it by \(a^2\):

$$\begin{equation} a^2S_{a^o}(n) = a^3 + a^5 + a^7 + a^9 + \ldots + a^{2n + 1} \end{equation}$$

Subtract (5) from (6):

$$-\,\left\{\begin{array}{lclllllllll} a^2S_{a^o}(n) & = & a^3 & + & a^5 & + & a^7 & + & \ldots & + & a^{2n + 1}\\ S_{a^o}(n) & = & a & + & a^3 & + & a^5 & + & \ldots & + & a^{2n - 1} \end{array}\right.$$ $$(a^2 - 1)S_{a^o}(n) = a^3 + a^5 + a^7 + \ldots + a^{2n + 1} - a - a^3 - a^5 - \ldots - a^{2n - 1} =$$ $$a^3 - a^3 + a^5 - a^5 + a^7 - a^7 + \ldots + a^{2n - 1} - a^{2n - 1} + a^{2n + 1} - a$$

and observe that all the members with like powers will cancel each other out except for the last two:

$$(a^2 - 1)S_{a^o}(n) = a^{2n + 1} - a = a(a^{2n} - 1)$$

and:

$$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{\sum_{i = 1}^n a^{2i - 1} = a \frac {a^{2n} - 1}{a^2 - 1}} \end{equation}$$


Problem 2.3

Find the sum of growing even powers of a constant integer base \(a > 1\):

$$\begin{equation} S_{a^e}(n) = \sum_{i = 1}^n a^{2i} = a^2 + a^4 + a^6 + \ldots + a^{2n} \end{equation}$$


Solution

At this point we should be able to compute (8) by analogy:

$$a^2S_{a^e}(n) = a^4 + a^6 + a^8 + a^{10} + \ldots + a^{2n + 2}$$ $$-\,\left\{\begin{array}{lclllllllll} S_{a^e}(n) \times a^2 & = & a^4 & + & a^6 & + & a^8 & + & \ldots & + & a^{2n + 2}\\ S_{a^e}(n) & = & a^2 & + & a^4 & + & a^6 & + & \ldots & + & a^{2n} \end{array}\right.$$ $$(a^2 - 1)S_{a^e}(n) = a^4 + a^6 + a^8 + \ldots + a^{2n + 2} - a^2 - a^4 - a^6 - \ldots - a^{2n} =$$ $$a^4 - a^4 + a^6 - a^6 + a^8 - a^8 + \ldots + a^{2n} - a^{2n} + a^{2n + 2} - a^2 =$$ $$a^2(a^{2n} - 1)$$

and:

$$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{\sum_{i = 1}^n a^{2i} = a^2 \frac {a^{2n} - 1}{a^2 - 1}} \end{equation}$$


Problem 2.4

Find the sum of inverse growing powers of a constant integer base \(a > 1\):

$$\begin{equation} S_{a^-}(n) = \sum_{i = 1}^n \frac {1}{a^i} = \frac {1}{a} + \frac {1}{a^2} + \frac {1}{a^3} + \ldots + \frac {1}{a^n} \end{equation}$$


Solution

Multiply both sides of (10) by \(a\):

$$\begin{equation} aS_{a^-}(n) = 1 + \frac {1}{a} + \frac {1}{a^2} + \ldots + \frac {1}{a^{n - 1}} \end{equation}$$

Subtract (10) from (11):

$$(a - 1)S_{a^-}(n) = 1 + \frac {1}{a} + \frac {1}{a^2} + \ldots + \frac {1}{a^{n - 1}} - \frac {1}{a} - \frac {1}{a^2} - \frac {1}{a^3} - \ldots - \frac {1}{a^n} =$$ $$1 + \frac {1}{a} - \frac {1}{a} + \frac {1}{a^2} - \frac {1}{a^2} + \ldots + \frac {1}{a^{n - 1}} - \frac {1}{a^{n - 1}} - \frac {1}{a^n} =$$ $$1 - \frac {1}{a^n}$$

and:

$$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{\sum_{i = 1}^n \frac {1}{a^i} = \frac {a^n - 1}{a^n(a - 1)}} \end{equation}$$

Further:

$$\frac {a^n - 1}{a^n(a - 1)} = \frac {1 - \frac {1}{a^n}}{a - 1}$$

and hence:

$$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{\lim_{n \to +\infty} \sum_{i = 1}^n \frac {1}{a^i} = \frac {1}{a - 1}} \end{equation}$$

By analogy we also obtain the results for odd:

$$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{\sum_{i = 1}^n \frac {1}{a^{2i - 1}} = \frac {a^{2n} - 1}{a^{2n - 1}(a^2 - 1)}} \end{equation}$$ $$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{\lim_{n \to +\infty} \sum_{i = 1}^n \frac {1}{a^{2i - 1}} = \frac {a}{a^2 - 1}} \end{equation}$$

and even powers:

$$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{\sum_{i = 1}^n \frac {1}{a^{2i}} = \frac {a^{2n} - 1}{a^{2n}(a^2 - 1)}} \end{equation}$$ $$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{\lim_{n \to +\infty} \sum_{i = 1}^n \frac {1}{a^{2i}} = \frac {1}{a^2 - 1}} \end{equation}$$


Problem 2.5

Find the sum of patterned inverse consecutive powers of a constant integer base \(a > 1\):

$$\begin{equation} S = \sum_{i = 1}^n \frac {i}{a^i} = \frac {1}{a} + \frac {2}{a^2} + \frac {3}{a^3} + \ldots + \frac {n}{a^n} \end{equation}$$


Solution

To calculate this sum we will use the combination of two approaches: rearrangement and reduction. First we will rearrange and then we will reduce.

Multiply both sides of (18) by \(a\):

$$\begin{equation} aS = 1 + \frac {2}{a} + \frac {3}{a^2} + \ldots + \frac {n}{a^{n - 1}} \end{equation}$$

Subtract (18) from (19):

$$aS - S = 1 + \frac {2}{a} + \frac {3}{a^2} + \ldots + \frac {n}{a^{n - 1}} - \frac {1}{a} - \frac {2}{a^2} - \frac {3}{a^3} - \ldots - \frac {n}{a^n} =$$ $$1 + \frac {2}{a} - \frac {1}{a} + \frac {3}{a^2} - \frac {2}{a^2} + \ldots + \frac {n}{a^{n - 1}} - \frac {n - 1}{a^{n - 1}} - \frac {n}{a^n} =$$ $$1 + \underbrace{\frac {1}{a} + \frac {1}{a^2} + \frac {1}{a^3} + + \ldots + \frac {1}{a^{n - 1}}}_{n - 1} - \frac {n}{a^n}$$

We observe that the terms in the middle of the above sum look almost like the sum (12) - almost because in (12) the index \(i\) runs from \(1\) to \(n\) while here \(i\) runs from \(1\) to \(n - 1\).

To make our current sum complete we add to and subtract from it the lacking term of \(a^{-n}\):

$$(a - 1)S = 1 + \underbrace{\frac {1}{a} + \frac {1}{a^2} + \frac {1}{a^3} + \ldots + \frac {1}{a^{n - 1}} + \frac {1}{a^n}}_{n} - \frac {1}{a^n} - \frac {n}{a^n}$$

The structure rearrangement portion is over since we have managed to reduce the current problem to the previously solved one. From (12) it follows:

$$(a - 1)S = 1 + \frac {a^n - 1}{a^n(a - 1)} - \frac {1}{a^n} - \frac {n}{a^n} =$$ $$1 + \frac {a^n - 1}{a^n(a - 1)} - \frac {1 + n}{a^n} =$$ $$\frac {a^n(a - 1)}{a^n(a - 1)} + \frac {a^n - 1}{a^n(a - 1)} - \frac {(1 + n)(a - 1)}{a^n(a - 1)} =$$ $$\frac {a^{n + 1} - a^n + a^n - 1 - a + 1 - na + n} {a^n(a - 1)} =$$ $$= \frac {a^{n + 1} - a(n + 1) + n}{a^n(a - 1)}$$

and:

$$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{\sum_{i = 1}^n \frac {i}{a^i} = \frac {a^{n + 1} - a(n + 1) + n}{a^n(a - 1)^2}} \end{equation}$$ $$\begin{equation} \bbox[#e8e8e8,3pt]{\lim_{n \to +\infty}\sum_{i = 1}^n \frac {i}{a^i} = \frac {a}{(a - 1)^2}} \end{equation}$$

\(\blacksquare\)
