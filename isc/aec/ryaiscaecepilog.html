Epilog

Reality Check

Being a part of a tutorial, the well-polished and smooth steps and source code presented here are no doubt idealistic to some degree but are representative nonetheless.

An activity absent from this discussion that, however, can not be avoided in practice - debugging - aids a deeper understanding of a problem at the current iteration and facilitates the success during the next one.


Exercises

To convince yourself that you have a firm grasp of the Shunting Yard algorithm add an exponentiation operator to AEC.


Potential

With an arbitrary naming convention aimed at reducing the token-recognition complexity AEC can be augmented to accept:

  • symbolic constants
  • variables
  • functions

as input.

The names of symbolic constants, for example, may be stipulated to be delineated with an underscore, ASCII decimal \(95\), character:

$$\_Pi\_ = 3.1415$$ $$\_E\_ = 2.718$$ $$\_EulersGamma\_ = 0.577215$$

The names of variables may be stipulated to be comprised of lower case letters only:

$$limit$$ $$area$$ $$volume$$

The names of functions may be stipulated to begin with an upper case letter:

$$Cos$$ $$Sin$$ $$Log$$

Constants and variables should be treated as operands while functions should be treated as operators of highest precedence.

Then, a second power of cosine of seven radians renders in an infix notation as follows:

$$E_{in} = Cos(7)\wedge 2$$

which renders in prefix and postfix notations as follows:

$$E_{pr} = [\wedge] [Cos] [7] [2]$$ $$E_{po} = [7] [Cos] [2] [\wedge]$$

On the other hand:

$$E_{in} = Cos(7\wedge 2)$$ $$E_{pr} = [Cos] [\wedge] [7] [2]$$ $$E_{po} = [7] [2] [\wedge] [Cos]$$

And:

$$E_{in} = Cos(7) \times 2$$ $$E_{pr} = [\times] [Cos] [7] [2]$$ $$E_{po} = [7] [Cos] [2] [\times]$$

Further:

$$E_{in} = Cos(7) \times_1 Tan(9) \times_2 Sin(11)$$ $$E_{pr} = [\times_2] [\times_1] [Cos] [7] [Tan] [9] [Sin] [11]$$ $$E_{po} = [7] [Cos] [9] [Tan] [\times_1] [11] [Sin] [\times_2]$$

And:

$$E_{in} = Cos(Tan(Sin(Cot(13))))$$ $$E_{pr} = [Cos] [Tan] [Sin] [Cot] [13]$$ $$E_{po} = [13] [Cot] [Sin] [Tan] [Cos]$$

and so on.

To demonstratie the abstract power of the Shunting Yard algorithm we will adjust it to operate on Boolean expressions consisting of human-readable strings as operands and logical operators NOT, AND, OR next.
